This week, we take a look at Shanghai’s AI Tower in the West Bund innovation cluster, part of Insider London’s China Programme. We ask what it shows us about the next stage of artificial intelligence. 

#### From waterfront regeneration to AI cluster

The West Bund sits on the Huangpu riverside in Shanghai’s Xuhui District. It is already known for culture, design and urban regeneration, but it is also becoming one of the city’s major AI districts.

At its centre is the [West Bund AI Valley](https://english.shanghai.gov.cn/en-SpecialIndustrialParks/20240202/2e3c00d502ef4845b61a75c2b8ab3ccb.html), a 32-hectare district with about 1.2 million square metres of construction area. The area is anchored by the West Bund International AI Tower and is designed to bring together AI companies, start-ups, research institutions and supply chain businesses.

A key part of the cluster is the [Shanghai Foundation Model Innovation Center](https://english.shanghai.gov.cn/en-Latest-WhatsNew/20250430/d641c50ef4f94163947dfbe27072c15e.html). Its models are the large AI systems behind many newer tools and applications. They can support language, images, audio, coding, research, customer service and other uses. They are not just individual products. They are platforms from which new products and businesses can be built.

The centre began with 21 foundation-model enterprises. Within 100 days, that number had passed 50. It is now home to more than 100 enterprises and has helped create a wider ecosystem of nearly 400 related companies in Xuhui. It has also supported 43 registered foundation models, around 61% of Shanghai’s total.

#### AI is no longer a general idea

Artificial intelligence has moved from specialist discussion into everyday business life. Companies now use AI in customer service, marketing, software development, finance, healthcare, logistics and product design. Students hear about it constantly, but often through headlines or tools on a screen.

The global picture shows how quickly this is changing. The [Stanford AI Index](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report) tracks the rapid growth of AI investment, model development and international competition. McKinsey’s [State of AI report](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai-how-organizations-are-rewiring-to-capture-value) found that AI is now being used across a wide range of business functions.

AI is everywhere in conversation, but hard to see. We see the outputs: a chatbot answer, a generated image, a translation tool, a recommendation, and a dashboard. We rarely see the system behind it: the companies, buildings, research teams, computing power, data, investment and policy support.

#### Making AI tangible

AI often feels abstract because its infrastructure is hidden. At West Bund, students can see how the pieces fit together. They can see the tower, the incubator, the product experience space and the wider cluster of companies and institutions around it.

The centre’s [AI product experience space](https://english.shanghai.gov.cn/en-IndustrialInnovation-Scitechcenter/20250718/ee9d651b99ef4f7f9d205f231a4a0d2d.html) includes applied technologies such as smart wearables, translation devices, health tools, audio products and interactive demonstrations. These are not just concepts. They are attempts to turn AI into products people can use, test, sell and improve.
