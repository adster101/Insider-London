This week, we take a look at **Shanghai’s AI Tower** in the West Bund innovation cluster, part of Insider London’s China Programme. The visit explores how artificial intelligence, MedTech, robotics and immersive technologies are being developed through a wider ecosystem of companies, universities, start-ups and applied demonstrations.

#### From waterfront regeneration to technology ecosystem

The West Bund sits on the Huangpu riverside in Shanghai’s Xuhui District. It is already known for culture, design and urban regeneration, and it is now also becoming one of the city’s major technology districts.

At its centre is the [West Bund AI Valley](https://english.shanghai.gov.cn/en-SpecialIndustrialParks/20240202/2e3c00d502ef4845b61a75c2b8ab3ccb.html), a 32-hectare district with about 1.2 million square metres of construction area. The area is anchored by the West Bund International AI Tower and brings together AI companies, start-ups, research institutions and supply chain businesses.

The wider area is also home to, or closely linked with, major technology companies including Alibaba, Tencent, Huawei, Microsoft and SenseTime. Their presence gives the district depth: large firms, specialist start-ups, university research, young talent and public planning all sit within the same innovation environment.

#### The Foundation Model Innovation Center

A key part of the cluster is the [Shanghai Foundation Model Innovation Center](https://english.shanghai.gov.cn/en-Latest-WhatsNew/20250430/d641c50ef4f94163947dfbe27072c15e.html). Foundation models are the large AI systems behind many newer tools and applications, supporting areas such as language, images, audio, coding, research and customer service.

The centre began with 21 foundation-model enterprises. Within 100 days, that number had passed 50. It is now home to more than 100 enterprises and has helped create a wider ecosystem of nearly 400 related companies in Xuhui. It has also supported 43 registered foundation models, around 61% of Shanghai’s total.

The centre supports companies with computing power, data, finance, talent and application matchmaking. In practical terms, it helps young companies move from research and early ideas towards products, users and commercial growth.

#### How the visit works

The visit begins on the 45th floor of the AI Tower, with an introduction to the area’s regeneration programme and the companies based in and around the cluster. From this vantage point, the group can understand West Bund as a planned innovation district, where property, policy, universities and technology companies are being connected.

The programme then includes a tour of the [Hong Kong University of Science and Technology Shanghai Center](https://hkust.edu.hk/news/opening-hkust-shanghai-center-xuhui-district), based in the AI Tower. This part of the visit looks at how HKUST acts as an incubator for AI, MedTech and other cutting-edge start-ups, and how academic talent works alongside technology companies.

The group also hears from start-up founders based in the space. These presentations demonstrate current technologies in areas such as robotics, virtual reality and AI, giving students a more detailed view of how new ideas are developed, tested and prepared for market.

#### Start-ups inside the space

Two start-ups help show the range of work taking place in the cluster.

[QXD Medical Technology](https://quanxiaode.com/en/) works on organ-level medical AI. Its Inner X-World platform uses images, records and signals to build patient-level organ models, forecast disease development and compare possible intervention pathways. It gives the group a clear example of AI being applied to MedTech and clinical decision-making.

[Orka](https://www.hiorka.com) works in AI-powered hearing technology. Its approach combines hardware, AI algorithms and hearing services to improve how hearing aids separate voices from noise and support remote fitting. This shows how AI can be built into physical products used in everyday life.

Together, examples such as QXD and Orka help the group understand the cluster as a working environment where AI connects with healthcare, hardware, robotics, immersive technology and consumer applications.
