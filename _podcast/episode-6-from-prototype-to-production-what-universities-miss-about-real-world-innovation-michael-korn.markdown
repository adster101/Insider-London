Our latest episode of The Knowledge Exchange features Michael Korn, the visionary founder of Blue Garage and a staunch advocate for hands-on learning, commercial insight, and collaborative design. With a career spanning healthcare innovation, physical prototyping, and scaling hardware in complex environments, Michael brings a wealth of experience to the table.

In this engaging conversation, Michael illuminates the strengths and shortcomings of university programs in preparing students for the unpredictable, creative, and commercial challenges of product development. He offers compelling insights into the "valley of death" that exists between prototype and scale, emphasising the crucial need for commercial skills to complement technical expertise. Moreover, he discusses how immersive real-world experiences can catalyse unexpected and rewarding career paths. Don't miss this opportunity to gain valuable perspectives from an industry leader!
